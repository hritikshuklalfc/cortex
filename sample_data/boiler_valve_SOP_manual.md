# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)
## High-Pressure Steam Valve Isolation and Replacement

**Document ID:** SOP-MECH-4402
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2026
**Equipment Type:** Boiler Main Steam Stop Valves (Gate/Globe)

### 1. Purpose
This procedure defines the mandatory safety and mechanical steps required for the safe isolation, depressurization, and replacement of high-pressure steam valves within the primary boiler loop.

### 2. Pre-Requisites and Safety Protocols
* **Permit to Work (PTW):** A valid Hot Work and Confined Space permit (if applicable) must be authorized by the Shift Charge Engineer.
* **PPE:** High-temperature resistant gloves, face shield, Nomex coveralls, and Class-E hard hat.
* **LOTO (Lockout/Tagout):**
  * Isolate the upstream and downstream manual isolation valves.
  * Apply physical padlocks and danger tags.
  * Disable the motorized actuator feed from the Motor Control Center (MCC).

### 3. Isolation and Depressurization
1. Verify the boiler load is reduced and steam flow is diverted.
2. Slowly open the localized vent and drain valves to bleed residual steam pressure.
3. Observe the pressure gauge on the spool piece until it reads exactly 0 PSIG.
4. Keep the drain and vent valves locked open during the entire maintenance window.

### 4. Valve Replacement Procedure
1. Disconnect the pneumatic/electrical connections from the actuator.
2. Unbolt the flanged connections using a hydraulic torque wrench, alternating in a star pattern to prevent uneven stress.
3. Remove the old valve using the overhead gantry crane (SWL 2 Tons).
4. Inspect and clean the flange faces. Install new spiral wound graphite gaskets.
5. Lower the new valve into place, aligning the flow direction arrow with the steam flow.
6. Hand-tighten all studs and nuts. Torque to 850 Nm in a star pattern.
7. Reconnect actuator and perform stroke test (0-100%).

### 5. Return to Service
1. Close the drain and vent valves.
2. Remove LOTO devices.
3. Slowly crack open the upstream isolation valve (10%) to equalize pressure and warm the line (preventing water hammer).
4. Inspect for leaks. If none, fully open the isolation valves and restore automatic control.
